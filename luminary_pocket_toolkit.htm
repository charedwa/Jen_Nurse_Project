Emergency Supply Checklist
Based on Medicare LCDs Source: CMS Medicare Coverage Database
General Eligibility (All Diagnoses)

Patient may have life expectancy ≤6 months if exhibiting:

  • Progressive disease — worsening clinical status, symptoms, labs
  • Unintentional weight loss ≥10% in past 6 months
  • Declining serum albumin or cholesterol
  • Dysphagia → recurrent aspiration / inadequate PO intake
  • Decline in KPS or PPS due to disease progression
  • Increased dependence with ADLs
  • Progressive stage 3–4 pressure ulcers despite optimal care
  • Increasing ER visits, hospitalizations, or MD visits related to primary dx
Diagnosis-Specific Criteria
🧠
Dementia / Alzheimer's
FAST 7C or beyond
▼
FAST Stage 7C+ (Alzheimer's)
  • Speech limited to ≤6 intelligible words
  • Unable to ambulate without assistance
  • Unable to sit up independently
Plus ≥1 complication in past 12 months:
  • Aspiration pneumonia
  • Pyelonephritis
  • Septicemia
  • Multiple stage 3–4 pressure ulcers
  • Recurrent fever after antibiotics
  • Weight loss ≥10% in 6 months or albumin <2.5 g/dL
❤️
Heart Disease / CHF
NYHA Class IV
▼
Primary Criteria
  • NYHA Class IV — symptoms at rest
  • Unable to perform minimal activity without dyspnea or angina
  • Optimally treated (diuretics, vasodilators, ACE-I, hydralazine, nitrates)
  • Angina at rest, resistant to nitrates; not candidate or declined invasive procedures
Supporting Documentation
  • EF <20%
  • Treatment-resistant symptomatic dysrhythmias
  • History of cardiac syncope or CVA from cardiac embolism
  • History of cardiac resuscitation
Signs/Symptoms

Orthopnea • PND • Dependent edema • Syncope • Weakness • Chest pain • Diaphoresis • Cachexia • JVD • Rales • Hepatomegaly

🫁
Pulmonary / COPD
Disabling dyspnea at rest
▼
  • Disabling dyspnea at rest, poorly responsive to bronchodilators
  • Bed-to-chair existence, fatigue, cough
  • Increasing ED visits/hospitalizations for pulmonary infections or respiratory failure
Documentation (past 3 months)
  • Resting arterial hypoxemia: pO₂ <55 mmHg or O₂ sat <88%
  • Hypercapnia: pCO₂ >50 mmHg
Supporting
  • Cor pulmonale and right heart failure
  • Unintentional progressive weight loss
🎗️
Cancer
PPS ≤70%
▼
  • Disease with metastases at presentation
  • Progression to metastatic disease with continued decline despite therapy
  • Patient declines further disease-directed therapy
  • Certain cancers (SCLC, brain, pancreatic) may qualify without other criteria
  • PPS ≤70%
🧬
Stroke / Coma
PPS ≤40%
▼
  • PPS ≤40%
  • Weight loss >10% in 6 months or >7.5% in 3 months
  • Serum albumin <2.5 g/dL
  • Pulmonary aspiration not responsive to SLP
  • Dysphagia severe enough to prevent sustaining life; patient declines artificial nutrition/hydration
  • Sequential calorie counts documenting inadequate intake
Supplemental

Aspiration pneumonia • Upper UTI • Sepsis • Refractory decubitus (3–4) • Recurrent fever after antibiotics

⚡
ALS
Critically impaired breathing
▼
Breathing Impairment (any of)
  • Dyspnea at rest
  • Vital capacity <30%
  • Supplemental O₂ required at rest
  • Declines artificial ventilation
Rapid Disease Progression
  • Independent ambulation → wheelchair/bed-bound
  • Normal → barely intelligible/unintelligible speech
  • Normal → pureed diet
  • Independent ADLs → major assistance in all ADLs
Severe Nutritional Impairment
  • Oral intake insufficient to sustain life
  • Continuing weight loss; dehydration/hypovolemia
  • Absence of artificial feeding
🔬
HIV Disease
CD4 <25 + viral load >100K
▼
  • CD4+ <25 cells/mm³
  • Viral load >100,000 copies/mL
  • PPS <50%
Plus ≥1 of:

CNS lymphoma • Wasting >33% LBM (untreated/refractory) • MAC bacteremia • PML • Systemic lymphoma • Refractory visceral KS • Renal failure without dialysis • Refractory cryptosporidium • Refractory toxoplasmosis

Supplemental

Chronic diarrhea ≥1 year • Albumin <2.5 g/dL • Age >50 • Advanced AIDS dementia • CHF at rest • Advanced liver disease

🫀
Liver Disease
Synthetic liver failure
▼
Synthetic Liver Failure
  • PT >5 seconds over control or INR >1.5
  • Serum albumin <2.5 g/dL
Plus ≥1 of:
  • Ascites refractory to treatment
  • Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis
  • Hepatorenal syndrome (elevated Cr/BUN, oliguria <500 mL/day, urine Na <10 mEq)
  • Hepatic encephalopathy refractory to treatment
  • Recurrent variceal bleeding despite therapy
Supplemental

Progressive malnutrition • Muscle wasting • Continued alcoholism • Liver cancer • Hep B or C refractory to interferon

🧪
Parkinson's Disease
Rapid progression + complications
▼
Rapid Progression (same criteria as ALS)
  • Ambulation → wheelchair/bed-bound
  • Normal → unintelligible speech
  • Normal → pureed diet
  • Independent → major assist in all ADLs
Breathing (any of)
  • Dyspnea at rest
  • VC <30% predicted
  • Supplemental O₂ at rest
  • Declines artificial ventilation
Complications (past 12 months)

Recurrent aspiration pneumonia • Pyelonephritis • Recurrent fever after antibiotics • Stage 3–4 decubitus

🫁
Dyspnea
Shortness of breath management
▼
Suggested Pharmacologic
  • Morphine 2.5–5 mg PO/SC q1h PRN (reduces air hunger)
  • Lorazepam 0.5–1 mg PO/SL q6h PRN if anxiety present
  • Oxygen if hypoxic or symptomatic
This medicine will help ease their breathing and make them feel calmer.
Non-Pharmacologic
  • High Fowler's (60–90°) or tripod position
  • Handheld or room fan directed at face (trigeminal nerve → reduces dyspnea)
  • Pursed-lip breathing: inhale nose, exhale slowly through pursed lips
  • Open window for fresh air if possible
  • Cool room temperature
  • Calming presence; minimize stimulation during episodes
  • Guided imagery, relaxation scripts
💧
Terminal Secretions
Death rattle
▼
Suggested Pharmacologic
  • Atropine 1% ophthalmic drops: 1–2 drops SL q1h PRN
  • Glycopyrrolate 0.1–0.2 mg SC q4h PRN
  • Scopolamine patch 1.5 mg q72h (slow onset — hours)
Non-Pharmacologic
  • Turn patient to side (right preferred) — gravitational drainage
  • Elevate HOB slightly
  • Minimize oral intake (actively dying)
  • Oral swabs for comfort, not stimulation
  • Suction only if secretions visible in mouth — avoid over-suctioning
This sound is common at this time. It's not uncomfortable for your loved one, but we're here to make sure they're at peace.
😰
Delirium & Terminal Agitation
Crisis management
▼
Signs

Confusion, vocal outbursts, moaning, pacing, fidgeting, screaming, thrashing, striking out, pulling lines, spiritual distress.

Suggested Pharmacologic
  • Haloperidol 0.5–2 mg PO/SC q1–2h PRN (start low, titrate)
  • ABH Gel 1 mL q4h PRN
  • Lorazepam 0.5–1 mg q4h PRN (only if anxiety component)
  • Consider: olanzapine, chlorpromazine, or methotrimeprazine if unresponsive
Avoid benzos alone in hypoactive delirium — may worsen symptoms.
Non-Pharmacologic
  • Soft lighting; limit noise; familiar objects
  • Soft, slow, calm voice: "You're safe. I'm here. You're in your room."
  • Do not restrain physically; use low beds and floor mats
  • Address pain, full bladder/bowel, thirst, fever
  • Soft music, aromatherapy (lavender, chamomile), warm blanket
This behavior can be a normal part of the body shutting down. We're making sure they are comfortable and peaceful.
🤢
Nausea & Vomiting
Assessment and management
▼
If actively vomiting: Turn on side to prevent aspiration.
Suggested Pharmacologic
  • Haloperidol 0.5–1 mg PO/SC q6–8h
  • Metoclopramide 10 mg PO/SC q6h
  • Ondansetron 4–8 mg PO/ODT q8h (less effective for opioid-induced)
Non-Pharmacologic
  • Head elevated 30–45° after meals/meds
  • Cool, well-ventilated room; dim lighting
  • Avoid strong odors
  • Small sips of clear liquids; ice chips; bland foods
  • Aromatherapy: peppermint, ginger, lemon
  • Acupressure P6 point (3 finger-widths below wrist, inner forearm)
  • Cool compress to forehead or back of neck
  • SL/SC/PR routes when oral intake limited
💩
Constipation
Prevention and treatment
▼
Prevention
  • Senna-S 1 tab PO BID
  • Docusate sodium 100 mg PO BID
Treatment
  • Bisacodyl suppository or enema
  • Lactulose 15–30 mL PO daily
  • Magnesium citrate or PEG if severe
Non-Pharmacologic
  • Upright position for toileting; commode preferred over bedpan
  • Warm beverages (prune juice, warm lemon water)
  • Fiber-rich foods if patient is mobile and hydrated
  • Abdominal massage: clockwise circular motion
  • Toileting time after meals (gastrocolic reflex)
  • Intervene if no BM in 48–72 hours
🌡️
Fever
Comfort-focused management
▼
Suggested Pharmacologic

Acetaminophen 500–1000 mg PO/PR q6h PRN

Non-Pharmacologic
  • Remove excess clothing/blankets; lightweight breathable sheets
  • Cool compress to forehead, neck, wrists
  • Tepid sponge bath (lukewarm — avoid cold or alcohol rubs)
  • Frequent small sips; ice chips; popsicles
  • Semi-upright positioning (30–45°)
  • Fan or open window
The fever is part of the body's natural response. We're using comfort measures to help them feel at ease.
🩸
Terminal Bleed
Massive hemorrhage protocol
▼
Most common: Head/neck, lung, or GI cancers. Sudden profuse bleeding from mouth, nose, trach, or rectum.
Emergency Response
  • DO NOT leave patient alone
  • Apply dark towels to bleeding site (absorbs discretely)
  • Administer Midazolam or Lorazepam 2 mg SL/SC
  • Administer Morphine 5–10 mg SC or SL
  • Gently guide family out if needed
  • Document time of event, comfort measures, death if occurred
  • Notify physician and supervisor immediately
We're focusing on keeping your loved one comfortable right now.
🩹
Skin Breakdown / Pressure Injury
▼
  • Reposition q2h
  • Keep skin clean and dry
  • Use pressure-relieving surfaces
  • Apply barrier creams
  • Treat infections topically or systemically as appropriate

Hand Hygiene

Hand Hygiene Protocol
CDC/WHO guidelines • 42 CFR §418.60
▼
When to Wash
  • Before and after every patient contact
  • Before donning and after doffing gloves
  • After contact with bodily fluids, secretions, or excretions
  • Before handling medications or equipment
Soap & Water Steps
  • Wet hands with warm water
  • Apply soap, lather ≥20 seconds — all surfaces including between fingers and under nails
  • Rinse thoroughly; dry with clean towel
  • Use towel to turn off faucet
Hand Sanitizer (≥60% alcohol)

Rub vigorously 15–20 seconds until dry. Not effective if hands are visibly soiled.

Aide Plan of Care Compliance
Per CMS 418.76 Requirements
  • POC must be written by RN, reviewed and updated every 14 days
  • POC must include frequency, tasks, and individualized instructions
  • Aide provides care exactly as ordered
  • Report to RN: refusal of care, skin breakdown, new symptoms
Documentation Example

"Per care plan: Assisted with full bed bath, peri-care, applied barrier cream to coccyx. Skin intact. No refusal of care. Patient in good spirits."

💡 Aide visits are frequently scrutinized during survey — ensure accurate visit times, clear documentation, and compliance with ordered frequencies.
